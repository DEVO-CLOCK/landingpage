What happens at sessions

Every session has a point.

No filler. Sessions rotate between four formats depending on the week.

01 · Hack Nights
Open build sessions
Everyone works on something — solo or paired. Ends with a quick show-and-tell round. Great for accountability and momentum.
02 · Skill Drops
Hands-on workshops
Deep dives on tools that matter: Git, building with APIs, AI tools, freelancing basics. Led by members or guests.
03 · Demo Days
Show what you built
Members present real projects to the group. Constructive feedback, exposure, and practice pitching — all in a supportive room.
04 · Guest Sessions
Industry, unfiltered
Developers, founders, and tech professionals share what they actually learned. Real conversation, not keynote theatre.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Are sessions really free? +
Yes. The biweekly in-person hangouts are completely free and open to any student. Membership is a separate, optional upgrade for people who want more structure and outcomes.
What's the difference between attending and membership? +
Attending is free — you come to sessions, learn, and network. Membership (₦7k/semester) adds a portfolio project guarantee, freelance referrals, CV review, mentorship pairing, access to resources and exclusive hangouts
Do I need to know how to code? +
Not at all. We welcome total beginners. The only requirement is genuine interest and showing up consistently.
How do the freelance referrals work? +
When the club sources client work, paying members get first access. The club takes a small facilitation cut, you keep the rest. We support you through pitching and delivery — especially on the first one.
How does Dev O'Clock for Schools work? +
We run structured tech bootcamps and workshops for secondary school and university students, facilitated by our members. Schools or student unions can partner with us to bring the programme on-site. Reach out via the form above.
